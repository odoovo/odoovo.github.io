Odoo 20 Roadmap: Key Features Your Business Should Watch

The vision for Odoo 20 revolves around three ideas: simple, intuitive, and a true simplifier for daily operations.


Key directions include:
  • Cleaner, more user‑friendly interface on both web and mobile, so new users can get productive faster.
  • More automation for repetitive work in accounting, sales, marketing, POS, and operations.
  • Better performance with fewer clicks and more focus on the screens users rely on every day.

Major feature highlights by module

POS: Making hardware easy.

In Odoo 20, the Point of Sale app is heavily upgraded to support retail, F&B, and multi‑store operations.

Highlighted improvements:
  • New hardware support for printers, scales, and payment terminals.
  • Faster order processing and better offline support.
  • Improved inventory management and reporting.

Website & eCommerce: Faster, more SEO‑ready

The Website and eCommerce modules in Odoo 20 are focused on performance, SEO, and a better design experience for marketers.

With these improvements, Odoovo can deliver SEO‑friendly websites, landing pages, and blogs on Odoo 20 quickly, without custom coding.

Key upgrades:
  • Faster page loading and better asset handling, so websites feel more responsive even with rich content.
  • SEO enhancements such as microdata (JSON‑LD), improved sitemaps, canonical URLs, no‑index options, and redirects.
  • Refreshed blog layout, themes, sidebars, and website setup wizards to make publishing easier.
  • New snippets like age‑restricted popups, WhatsApp buttons, stretched links, and grid layouts for landing pages.
  • More powerful forms: better validation, file upload rules, Country–State linking, extra donation fields, and copy‑to‑sender options.

Accounting & finance: More automation, more transparency

Odoo 20 introduces several features that help finance teams reduce manual work and increase accuracy.

These enhancements are particularly useful for companies dealing with multiple currencies, complex tax rules, and strict audit requirements.

Highlights include:
  • “Pay from Odoo” to trigger vendor and supplier payments directly from within Odoo using integrated payment providers.
  • A public “Accounting localization” website with standard charts of accounts, taxes, and configuration guidelines for each country.
  • Flexible handling of tax‑inclusive and tax‑exclusive pricing on products with on‑the‑fly switching on documents.
  • Multiple IDs per partner with type definitions and validity checks.
  • Bank reconciliation on any account with automatic adjustment entries when needed, including foreign currency corrections and cash‑basis tax adjustments.

HR, Timesheets, Payroll: Data‑driven workforce management

Odoo 20 brings a series of updates to HR, Payroll, and Timesheets to support data‑driven staff management.

With Odoo, companies can manage attendance, payroll, planning, and field service in a single integrated Odoo environment.

Key changes:
  • Payroll gets a clearer dashboard and a dedicated Pay Runs view to reduce manual processing.
  • Timesheets no longer rely on separate timers; duration behaves like a live timer and synchronizes with Attendance.
  • The Timesheet Assistant, powered by ActivityWatch, tracks local device activity (stored locally for privacy) and suggests timesheet entries based on user behavior.
  • Field Service is merged into Planning, enabling multi‑resource assignments, image attachments, task links, and break management in one place.

CRM, Marketing & Automation: True multichannel engagement

The CRM and Marketing apps in Odoo 20 continue to evolve to support multichannel customer engagement.

This allows businesses to build a fully integrated marketing‑to‑sales funnel within Odoo, from lead capture to deal closing.

Notable improvements:
  • CRM enhancements covering multiple pipelines, improved auto‑assignment, and a modern email plugin for sales teams.
  • Email Marketing and Marketing Automation receive new building blocks, templates, dynamic mailing lists, easier contact management, and support for larger attachments.
  • Social Marketing supports Stories, scheduled first comments, and new automation actions for long‑term campaigns.
  • Marketing Automation gets a clearer interface to design complex customer journeys.
